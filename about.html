My Story

I've been part of the Endless Online community for years — drawn in by the game's unique charm, its pixel art style, and the dedicated player base that has kept it alive long past its expected lifespan.

What started as playing the game evolved into curiosity about how it worked under the hood. I began reverse-engineering the protocol, studying the GFX format, and experimenting with custom server builds. That curiosity snowballed into the projects you see on this blog.

EO Community Involvement

Over the years I've been involved in multiple facets of the EO ecosystem — from playing on custom servers to running my own, contributing to community tooling, and building new client experiences from scratch.

My work spans server-side development, client development in Java and JavaScript, map editing tooling, and pixel art assets created specifically for the EO format.

Open Source

I believe strongly in open-source development and knowledge sharing. The EO community has benefited enormously from developers who documented the protocol and built the early tools. I aim to continue that tradition.
